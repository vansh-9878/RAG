--- Questions and Answers ---
Q: What is the grace period for premium payment under the National Parivar Mediclaim Plus Policy?
A: The grace period for premium payment under the National Parivar Mediclaim Plus Policy is thirty days.

Q: What is the waiting period for pre-existing diseases (PED) to be covered?
A: Expenses related to the treatment of a Pre-Existing Disease (PED) and its direct complications shall be excluded until the expiry of thirty six (36) months of continuous coverage after the date of inception of the first policy.

Q: Does this policy cover maternity expenses, and what are the conditions?
A: Yes, this policy covers maternity expenses, including medical treatment for childbirth (complicated deliveries and caesarean sections during hospitalization) and lawful medical termination of pregnancy during the Policy Period. The female Insured Person must be continuously covered for at least 24 months and be between 18 and 45 years of age, with a 24-month waiting period for delivery or termination (waived for accident-induced cases). Coverage is limited to two deliveries or terminations across the policy and its renewals, and only one per single Policy Period, excluding pre and post hospitalization expenses other than pre and post natal treatment, and generally excluding maternity expenses of a surrogate mother unless covered under infertility. Ectopic pregnancy is covered under 'In-patient treatment' if established by medical reports, though it is also listed as an exclusion.

Q: What is the waiting period for cataract surgery?
A: The waiting period for cataract surgery is two years.

Q: Are the medical expenses for an organ donor covered under this policy?
A: Yes, the medical expenses incurred for an organ donor's hospitalization during the policy period for harvesting the organ donated to an insured person are covered, provided the donation conforms to the Transplantation of Human Organs Act 1994, the organ is for an insured person medically advised to undergo transplant, expenses are for in-patient care, and a claim has been admitted under the In-patient Treatment Section for the insured person.

Q: What is the No Claim Discount (NCD) offered in this policy?
A: The No Claim Discount (NCD) offered is a flat 5% on the base premium for policies renewed with a one-year term, provided no claims were reported in the expiring policy. For policies exceeding one year, the aggregate NCD for each claim-free year is allowed on renewal, not exceeding a flat 5% of the total base premium for the policy term.

Q: Is there a benefit for preventive health check-ups?
A: Yes, expenses for health check-ups are reimbursed at the end of a block of two continuous policy years, provided the policy has been continuously renewed without a break, and are subject to limits stated in the Table of Benefits (e.g., up to INR 5,000, INR 7,500, or INR 10,000 every two years).

Q: How does the policy define a 'Hospital'?
A: A 'Hospital' is defined as any institution established for in-patient care and day care treatment of disease/injuries, registered with local authorities under the Clinical Establishments (Registration and Regulation) Act, 2010, or complying with specific minimum criteria including qualified nursing staff, a minimum number of inpatient beds, qualified medical practitioners, a fully equipped operation theatre, and daily patient record maintenance.

Q: What is the extent of coverage for AYUSH treatments?
A: The Company shall indemnify Medical Expenses incurred for Inpatient Care treatment under Ayurveda, Yoga and Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha and Homeopathy systems of medicines during each Policy Period up to the limit of Sum Insured as specified in the Policy Schedule in any AYUSH Hospital.

Q: Are there any sub-limits on room rent and ICU charges for Plan A?
A: Yes, for Plan A, room charges are limited to up to 1% of the Sum Insured (SI) or actual charges, whichever is lower, and ICU charges are limited to up to 2% of the SI or actual charges, whichever is lower.
